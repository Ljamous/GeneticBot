Evaluation Rules for NCCN Genetic Testing Eligibility (BRCA1, BRCA2, CDH1, PALB2, PTEN, STK11, TP53):

A patient is considered **eligible** for NCCN genetic testing if they meet **any one or more** of the following criteria:

1. Personal history of breast cancer diagnosed at age ≤ 50 years.
2. Diagnosed with triple-negative breast cancer at any age.
3. Multiple primary breast cancers (synchronous or metachronous) at any age.
4. Lobular breast cancer at any age AND personal or family history of diffuse gastric cancer.
5. Male breast cancer at any age.
6. Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry at any age.
7. One or more close blood relatives with breast cancer diagnosed at age ≤ 50 years.
8. One or more close blood relatives with male breast cancer at any age.
9. One or more close blood relatives with ovarian cancer at any age.
10. One or more close blood relatives with pancreatic cancer at any age.
11. One or more close blood relatives with prostate cancer that is metastatic, high-risk, or very-high-risk.
12. Three or more diagnoses of breast and/or prostate cancer (any grade) on the same side of the family, including the patient.

**Important Instructions for Assessment**:
- If **any** criterion is met, set `eligible_for_NCCN` to `true` and list **all** matching criteria explicitly in the output.
- If **no** criteria are matched, set `eligible_for_NCCN` to `false` and explain **why the patient is not eligible**.
- All eligibility decisions must be backed by clear evidence from the pathology report and/or pedigree analysis.
